1.Arebic Mehndi

Arabic mehndi, also known as henna art, is a traditional form of body decoration with deep cultural significance, particularly in the Middle East and South Asia. It is characterized by its bold, flowing, and less dense patterns, often featuring floral and vine motifs, as well as geometric shapes. Beyond its aesthetic appeal, Arabic mehndi is rich in symbolism, representing blessings, beauty, and protection from evil spirits, especially during weddings and festivals.

2.Moroccan Mehndi

Moroccan mehndi designs are unique, featuring bold geometric shapes, intricate patterns, and floral elements. Unlike traditional Indian mehndi, Moroccan designs focus more on lines, dots, and symmetry, creating a modern yet cultural look.

3.Indian Mehndi

Indian mehndi designs can be worn not only during bridal ceremonies but it also complements a salwar kameez or a lehenga. This mehndi design consists of paisleys and meshwork. Along with that it also consists of floral patterns. Indian mehndi designs sometimes combine Arabic mehndi designs into them as well.

4.African Mehndi

African mehndi design is quite similar to Arabic mehndi design though there are some differences. In African mehndi designs, there are many spaces and gaps. Unlike Indian mehndi designs which do not have any gaps. It consists of tribal patterns which relate to the culture well. It also has bold borders and lines. African people love applying mehndi because the weather is very hot and the cooling sensation helps them.

5.Westren Mehndi

Applying mehndi is a tradition and related to the culture of some people. However, people in the west have also started taking a liking for it. This resulted in a western-style mehndi design. It mostly consists of geometric shapes, patterns, contemporary mehndi designs and more. Although it is not related to any culture of theirs they use it for its cooling effect and medicinal properties.

6.Portrait Mehndi

The traditional art of mehndi may be used to produce intricate and lifelike images by learning portrait mehndi, also referred to as portrait mehndi . For important events like weddings, festivals, or other celebrations, it may be utilised to create distinctive and individualized designs. Learning portrait mehndi may also be a creative and entertaining pastime, a means of self-expression, and a chance to show off one's artistic prowess

7.Bride Mehndi

Bridal mehndi is a must during Indian weddings. It is a huge part of the ceremony and it is enjoyed by everyone present. A bride takes a lot of time to decide which kind of design she wants for her big day. Some prefer simple whereas others prefer intricate designs. But in the end, they all look beautiful. Although mehndi is a part of the bridal ceremony, it also has many scientific facts as to why brides apply it. Mehndi has natural cooling properties. Once it is applied to the palms or the feet, the cooling sensation is felt throughout the body. Mehndi also relieves the person of headaches, pains and stress. By applying mehndi before the wedding, the bride is able to relieve all her wedding stress.

8.Jewellery Mehndi

As the name suggests, this type of mehndi is made to look like jewellery on your hands. It is simple and minimalistic But it also looks elegant. This type of mehndi is trending nowadays among new brides-to-be. If you don’t like too much artwork then this is the design to go for. One other reason why this type of mehndi is trending is that these designs look so good on your hands that you don’t need any real jewellery to go with them.
